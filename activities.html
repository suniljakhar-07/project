1. Paddy cultivation

After land preparation we had transplanted PR-121 Paddy on 30th June,2020. The crop was harvested 

on 10th October,2020 and yield was 24Quintal per acre. This is the first crop harvested organically.

For land preparation, we used cow dung manure and waste decomposer. Among the manures, we used 

Blue-green Algae and two times liquid goat manure prepared by using waste decomposer. Twice, we used 

Sagarika as growth promoter and occasionally NPK bacteria is used. Now, we are in the position 

to supply organic rice, both polish and unpolished in lower rate.

2. Banana cultivation

Our main emphasis is on horticulture and the initiative is started by planting Banana Grand Naine

variety on 21st July, 2020 for a acre of land. We have to face a lot of constrain for this crop, 

as the climatic condition is not much suited for it. But, our efforts is going on and gradually 

situation is improving. It is completely organic and without any artificial support such as crop 

cover, poly house etc. As it took more time for fruiting and proper growth, we had experimented 

inter cropping and it was successful. We had tried for  Moong, Lobia and Maize in the month of 

August-September, and successfully get good yield. We had used cowdung, goat and poultry manures 

only. As maize is very much prone to pest, we used Neem oil and herbal self made pesticides, 

and the result was excellent. Before, the onset of Winter, banana were is good conditions, but, 

harsh winter had definitely impact  and indication of frost is noticed and growth were almost 

seized. But, now (26th Januaruy,2021), it is gradually improving. We had also tried Cauliflower 

as inter crop, and we are happy to share that bumper yield is noticed. But, unfortunately, 

we are not able to sell it due to very less price. But, it was a blessing for us, fed to our 

goat and even poultry. The major crop i.e. Banana took more than a year’s span for fruiting, 

hence, we are expecting it  to come by the month of August onwards.

3. Garlic 

We are also planted garlic in the month of November, just after Paddy harvest. We had used
 
waste decomposer, liquid goat and cow dung manures only and crop is growing well. In the 

month of March, we are expecting the harvest of garlic.

4. Pea

Another cop sown in post harvest paddy field is Pea and it is growing well, but, weed

is creating problem, but, we are now in the position to manage it to a great extent.

5. Black Gram

Besides, Pea, we had also sown black gram, but due to profuse growth of weeds, it is not

doing well. We are started weeding and soon, it will take the pace of growth.

Besides, a number of crop is tried and we are getting satisfactory results, like
 
Onion, Radish, Brocolli, Carrot, Fava bean (Bakla).

6. Guava

Guava is also planted in the month of September, 2020 and plant is growing well. We are

hopeful that it will bring fruit in the month of July-August onward. The Guava plant
 
is of bushy variety of Thai and Taiwan origin plant.

7. Moringa

Drum stick or Moringa will be another vegetable (fruit and leaf) and fodder crop in our

organic venture. We had already planted few plants in the month of September, and these

plants are able to survive in spite of chilly winter. From March onward, new plantation
 
will be started.

8. Papaya

Papaya will be the other major crop of our venture, considering the people of eastern
 
and north eastern India, especially, as they are using green Banana as vegetable crop
 
also and frequently. Taking into account of its beneficial effects on liver and other
 
health benefit, it is our moral responsibility to provide this crop and also to 

popularize among the people of this regions.

GOAT FARMING

Eleven goats were brought to our farm on 16th August,2020 and our goat farming started.

We are not able to provide organic feed initially, but, gradually providing organic 

green fodder from 10th September onward. The grain is not completely organic. Hence,
 
we are providing semi organic feed at this moment. Within 3-4 month, we can provide 

organic feed only to our goat. On  6th September, nine goats were also added to our 

farm. We are even, not feeding them antibiotic also. Sixteen kids birth took place 

and due to harsh climatic condition, only nine kids survived and now they are in 

good conditions. Our aim of goat farming is to produce hormone free and without 

using junk food, lean goat for slaughters. Besides, producing goat milk also.

POULTRY

One hundred and fifty “Kadaknath” Chicks of 65-75 days old were brought to our farm on

7th July, and similarly like goat, started feeding non organic maize and other products

as a feed. From September, onward we are able to provide organic maize and other products.

Hence, our poultry is also semi organic fed. Use of antibiotic for feed is completely 

absent, even, during illness of chicks, only multivitamins were used. From December 

second week, egg laying started and now it is available for sell at our far.
